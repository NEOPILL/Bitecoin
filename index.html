Whitepaper

Bitecoin is designed as a usable commodity: a currency you can actually live on, not just trade on. The whitepaper lays out:

  • Why legacy banks are no longer neutral infrastructure.
  • How Bitecoin removes permission from everyday money.
  • A path for vendors to accept BITE without touching “crypto complexity”.

Full PDF and technical spec coming soon. This is version 0 of the public story.

“If I accept Bitecoin, how do I get paid?”

Vendors will be able to choose: hold BITE as an asset, or auto-convert to their local currency at checkout via integration partners.

You shouldn’t need to be a trader to run a café.

Tokenomics

Exact numbers are still being tuned, but the design goal is simple: enough supply to be used, scarce enough to matter.

  • Total supply: to be finalized after testnet + community review.
  • Founders’ allocation: transparent, vested, and publicly trackable.
  • No stealth mints, no surprise unlocks, no hidden admin keys.

A full breakdown with charts and vesting schedules will live here.

Design principle
Spendable  ≠  Inflatable

Bitecoin aims to behave like a unit of account and a store of work – not a lottery ticket.

For vendors & businesses

Today, banks can freeze or question your money before letting you use it. Bitecoin’s goal is to flip that: money you receive should be final and under your control.

  • Instant settlement, no “3–5 business days”.
  • Low, transparent fees for micro-payments and big invoices.
  • Simple integration for web shops, point-of-sale and subscriptions.

Replace the bank, not the business.

We’re building for normal people: salary, rent, groceries, bills. Bitecoin is meant to live next to your keys and wallet – not in a forgotten trading app.
